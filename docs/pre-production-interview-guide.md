# Pre-Production Buyer Interview Guide (T-004)

Companion to [`pre-production-buyer-onepager.md`](pre-production-buyer-onepager.md).
The one-pager states three persona hypotheses; this guide turns
them into questions designed to **falsify** the hypotheses fast.

Use this for 30-minute discovery calls with line producers, UPMs,
VFX supervisors / producers, and (as user-influencers)
showrunners or writers' assistants.

Target outcome: after 10 interviews, the one-pager has either
been confirmed or substantively rewritten.

---

## Logistics (read aloud at the top, ~30 seconds)

> "Thanks for the time. I'm researching how pre-production teams
> handle script breakdown and continuity today, ahead of building
> something that might help. I'm not selling anything. Five
> minutes from now if this isn't relevant to your role, please
> kick me. Otherwise, I'd love 25 minutes of your time."

Record only with explicit consent. Take handwritten notes
otherwise — the friction discourages auto-paraphrasing.

---

## Five buyer-validation questions

Each question is designed so the answer **falsifies** one of the
one-pager's hypotheses.

1. **"Walk me through the last time you signed off on a tool
   purchase for a production. What was it, how was it paid for,
   and who else had to approve?"**
   Tests: does the interviewee actually own the budget line, or
   are they a specifier? Determines whether they're the buyer
   or an influencer.

2. **"On your last production, how did the script breakdown
   actually happen — who did it, what tools did they use, and
   what part of the process took the most calendar time?"**
   Tests: is breakdown a labour problem or a tool problem? If
   the answer is "the coordinator was great, we just needed two
   more weeks", we're selling labour augmentation, not
   software. If "the tool kept losing data" or "we
   re-keyed the script three times", we're selling tooling.

3. **"What's the biggest budget surprise you've had in
   pre-production in the last 18 months?"**
   Tests: does Fabula's value-prop hit the actual blowout
   pattern? If the surprise was crew overtime or location
   scheduling, the narrative-graph pitch doesn't help. If it
   was missing-shot scope or continuity errors, it does.

4. **"If you could buy a turnkey solution that produced your
   first-draft breakdown sheet plus a continuity-error report
   automatically from the script PDF, what would you pay per
   show? Range, not a number."**
   Tests: willingness-to-pay anchor. If they balk at the
   premise, the persona is wrong. If they give a number under
   $5k, mid-market only. Over $20k, studio tier.

5. **"Who on the production tends to bring in new software, and
   what makes them champion something? Who blocks it?"**
   Tests: identifies the actual champion-blocker pair for
   land-and-expand. Often surfaces that the buyer is two
   people: an enthusiast at the working level and a gatekeeper
   at the executive level.

---

## Three "show me your workflow" prompts

Get the interviewee narrating their last cycle. Real friction
surfaces in the verbs, not the noun lists.

6. **"Pull up the Movie Magic Scheduling (or whatever tool) file
   from your last show. Walk me through how a scene gets from
   the script PDF into that file."**
   Watch where they pause, swear, or apologise for a hack.
   Those are the real product opportunities.

7. **"Show me how you check continuity today — character
   placement, prop continuity, callback shots. What does the
   workflow look like from 'I noticed something' to 'I fixed
   it'?"**
   Tests whether continuity validation is even on the
   interviewee's mental model. If they say "the script
   supervisor handles it on set", we're selling to the wrong
   role.

8. **(VFX-specific)** **"For your last bid, walk me through how
   you got from the script to a vendor-ready scope sheet. What
   was the worst part?"**
   Tests the VFX-Producer hypothesis. The answer is almost
   certainly "missing shots that nobody flagged until the cut" —
   if it's anything else, the VFX persona needs reframing.

---

## Two close-out questions

9. **"If you tried a product that did [the specific thing they
   just complained about most], what would it have to show you
   in week one for you to keep using it past the trial?"**
   Forces them to name the activation event. Real numbers
   ("first breakdown sheet in under 4 hours", "continuity report
   that catches at least one real error") are gold. Vague
   answers ("save me time") flag a persona that doesn't really
   have the pain.

10. **"Who else on the production would I need to talk to, to
    understand how a decision like this gets made?"**
    Standard snowball-sampling close. Also surfaces the
    champion-blocker pair from question 5 by name.

---

## Post-interview synthesis

Within 24 hours, score the interview against:

- **Persona match**: 1–3 from the one-pager. Did the
  interviewee fit one of the three personas, or do we need a
  fourth?
- **Hypothesis confirmation**: which of the five claims in the
  one-pager were strengthened? Which were weakened?
- **Open questions resolved**: any of the five open questions in
  the one-pager closed?
- **Quote candidates**: 1–3 verbatim quotes that could appear in
  marketing copy (with permission).

After 10 interviews, rewrite the one-pager.
